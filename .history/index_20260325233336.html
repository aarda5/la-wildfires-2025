6 Facts About the LA Wildfires

January 2025 — Los Angeles, California

The Facts

  1. 01

    57,529 Acres Burned

    Fourteen wildfires swept across Los Angeles from January 7–31, 2025, scorching over 57,000 acres of urban, canyon, and residential land.

  2. 02

    18,000+ Structures Destroyed

    More than 18,000 homes and buildings were destroyed. The Palisades Fire alone destroyed 6,837 structures — the most destructive wildfire in LA history.

  3. 03

    Winds Reached 100 mph

    Hurricane-force Santa Ana winds drove the fires' explosive spread. The region had also gone eight months without measurable rainfall beforehand.

  4. 04

    200,000+ People Evacuated

    More than 200,000 residents were forced to flee their homes. At peak, over 414,000 Southern California Edison customers also lost power.

  5. 05

    ~440 Estimated Deaths

    The official confirmed toll is 31, but a JAMA study (August 2025) estimated around 440 deaths when accounting for smoke exposure, disrupted healthcare, and stress-related mortality.

  6. 06

    Up to $131 Billion in Damage

    Total economic losses are estimated between $76B and $131B, placing these fires among the ten costliest disasters in U.S. history. Climate change increased the likelihood of such conditions by 35%.
